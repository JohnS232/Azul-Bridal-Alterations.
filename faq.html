Frequently Asked Questions

Book your first bridal alteration 8-12 weeks before your wedding day. This allows ample time for a second fitting and any adjustments you may want to make before your big day.

For an accurate fitting, we highly recommend bringing all items you intend to wear on your wedding day. This includes undergarments, bustiers, corsets, shapewear, as well as your chosen necklace and earrings. Additionally, it is essential to bring the shoes you plan to wear to ensure the dress length is tailored to perfection. Your attention to these details will help us provide the best fit possible.

Typically, our fittings require a few visits. During your second fitting, we will ensure every detail is perfect, including the bust, waist, hips, hem, and bustle. Any adjustments can be made after the second fitting to guarantee the ideal fit. Unlike many of our competitors, we do not charge for additional fittings, ensuring a seamless and stress-free experience.

The first fitting is typically the longest, lasting around 45-60 minutes. Subsequent fittings, including the second and third, usually take 45 minutes or less to complete.

The entire alteration process can take several weeks, and may take longer during the peak wedding season (March-June). With an average of two fittings, this typically spans 6-8 weeks. Dresses requiring more extensive alterations, such as adjustments to straps, bust, hips, or bustle, may take additional time. For simpler alterations, like a hem, the process may be completed in as little as one week. We also offer a rush option for last-minute alterations and strive to accommodate every bride to the best of our ability.

Yes, we can certainly work with vintage dresses. Our skilled team is experienced in handling delicate fabrics and intricate designs, and we take extra care when making alterations to vintage pieces. However, depending on the condition of the dress, certain repairs may require more time and attention. We recommend scheduling a consultation to assess the dress and determine the best course of action.
